A little about us

Our Goals and Objectives are to:

  1. Manage the homeless cat population in a humane and effective way through our TNR work.
  2. Tame, socialise and care for rescued cats and kittens, and find them secure, loving, forever homes which have been vetted by experienced PCR volunteers.
  3. Provide information and educate the public about the welfare and care of both domestic and community cats.
  4. Support existing and potential cat owners by providing information and advice on a range of cat behavioural issues.
  5. Follow best practice in animal rescue and to train volunteers to assist our cat welfare and rescue work.
  6. Advocate for better animal welfare legislation, especially for cats, and for enforcement of existing legislation.
  7. Set up fostering programmes with vulnerable groups (the elderly, young people in care, individuals with intellectual and/or physical disabilities, individuals with mental ill health, offenders on parole, etc).
  8. Demonstrate that by caring for cats we learn to care better for ourselves.
  9. Promote that all life should be treated with respect, compassion and empathy.
  10. Be open and transparent in the work we do.

Since forming in August 2014, we have TNR’d over 1500 feral cats and we have rescued, tamed and found loving forever homes for over 2000 cats and kittens.

We have provided veterinary treatment for many more and we have rehomed a number of domestic cats who have been surrendered to us by their humans for a variety of reasons.

Our partner vets tell us they have seen a dramatic reduction in the number of homeless cats and unwanted kittens since we started our work.

We are in the process of applying for membership of the Association of Dogs and Cats Homes.

We successfully applied for charitable status with the Charities Regulatory Authority in 2016 (our registered charity number is 20105297).

We also received our tax exemption number (CHY 21973) from Revenue in 2017.

We hope you will help us by adopting, volunteering and/or donating!
